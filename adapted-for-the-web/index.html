I open my eyes to a ceiling I don’t know. But the Polaroid on the nightstand says:

“YOUR ROOM. DISCOUNT INN.”

So it’s mine. That’s how this works.

The phone rings. A steady voice asks what I remember. I tell him about The attack. My wife. The man in the bathroom. Two intruders. One I caught, one I didn’t. The missing piece of my life.

I stand in front of the mirror and read my skin:

FACT 1: MALE.
FACT 2: WHITE.
FACT 3: DRUG DEALER.
FACT 4: LICENSE PLATE — 1I7…
FACT 5: “JOHN G.”

Always John G. I repeat The lines until they feel like memory.

Teddy walks in without knocking. Friendly. Too friendly. He says he’s been helping me. He says we made a lot of progress yesterday. I don’t remember him from yesterday. But I have a Polaroid of him that says:

“DON’T BELIEVE HIS LIES.”

So I don’t.

I talk about Sammy. How he couldn’t make new memories. How his wife tested him. How she paid for it with her life. I tell the story because it keeps me steady — it’s the one memory I never lose. I don’t ask why.

Teddy says he found the guy. A drug dealer. A John G. He wants to take me to him. Says we’re close. Says this is the moment everything has been leading to. I want to believe him. But belief is a luxury I don’t have.

The Warehouse Approaches We drive into the abandoned building. The walls echo back our footsteps. Teddy hangs behind. He tells me to be careful. I put my hand on The tattoo over my heart:

“JOHN G. RAPED AND MURDERED MY WIFE.”

If the next room brings me closer to him, I’m ready. I walk forward —

Natalie Again, I taste blood and don’t know why. She stands over me, shaking, angry. She says things I don’t follow. Things I might have done. Things she knows I won’t remember doing. I want to say I’m sorry for something — anything — but I don’t even know what The apology is for.

Motel - Burt hands me two keys. Says I’m in two rooms. Says I asked for them. I tell him I don’t remember that. He shrugs — he’s seen this before. I check The notes taped inside The mirror frame:

“GET ROOM 21.”

“CHECK OTHER ROOM.”

Both written by me. Both equally unfamiliar.

Natalie gives me a scrap with a plate number. Says it’s connected to the man I’m chasing. I nod as if I understand. My fingers fold the paper neatly, like muscle memory doing the job my brain won’t.

At Natalie’s Bar. Natalie keeps her distance. Her voice is calm, but her eyes won’t stay still. She asks where Jimmy is. I tell her I don’t remember. Her jaw tightens. She doesn’t say it, but she hates me for something I can’t recall doing.

We’re in a Jaguar I don’t remember stealing. I flip The Polaroid over. “YOUR CAR.” My handwriting. I press The card between my fingers. If I wrote it, I must’ve known why. But The reason’s gone now, floating somewhere behind me on a road I can’t see.

Teddy Enters the Warehouse He walks in like he’s late to something important. He talks fast. Hands everywhere. He says the guy was Jimmy Grantz — drug dealer, small time, nervous type. He looks at me, then at the body. “Lenny, we did it,” he says. But his eyes flicker. Just once. I hold onto that.

Jimmy drops to his knees before I even know his name. The floor is cold. His breath turns shallow. I fire. I watch the life slip out, then reach for the Polaroid because that’s what I do — fix the moment before it fades in my head. The picture begins to develop. A body. A shirt soaked through. I write on the strip so the next version of me won’t hesitate:

“HE’S NOT THE ONE.”
